Customer Service

The top skills of a customer service representative: patience, attentiveness, knowledge, and positive attitude.

Patience

  • Use positive body and verbal language
  • Create a friendly enviornment
  • Do not let a heated customer force you to lose your cool

Attentiveness

  • The ability to really listen to customers is vital
  • Dealing wuth customers daily can get tiring on your end, but for them it is a new experience each time
  • Pay attention to individual customer interactions
  • Take note of their verbal and body language that they use to describe their problems
  • Be respectful of their feedback

Knowledge

  • The best representatives will have a deep knowledge of their company, product, enviornment
  • Without knowing the product they are having trouble with, you won't know how to help problem solve their problems.

Positive Attitude

  • The tone of your voice carries over the phone

Troubleshooting Basics

  1. Make sure your information is safe
    • safe = your data is still available to transfer if something happens to your current computer
  2. Review what you know
    • Write down notes on what occurred
  3. Establish a theory of probable cause
    • Ask more detailed questions that lead you to a guess as to what happened
  4. Test the theory to determine cause, retest as necessary
    • Test, re-test, test again
  5. Document findings, actions, and outcomes.

Clauses

Independent Clauses

  • Independent clauses are a final thought. They are able to stand on their own. An independent clause, also called a main clause, is a clause that can stand on its own. It contains all the information necessary to be a complete sentence. An independent clause has a subject that tells you what the sentence is about and a verb that tells you what the subject is doing. It expresses a complete thought, relaying that something has happened or was said.

Dependent Clauses

  • A dependent clause, also called a subordinate clause, is a clause that cannot stand on its own because it does not contain all the information necessary to be a complete sentence. A clause is dependent because of the presence of words such as before, after, because, since, in order to, although, and though).
