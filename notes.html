Customer Service

The top skills of a customer service representative: patience, attentiveness, knowledge, and positive attitude.

Patience

  • Use positive body and verbal language
  • Create a friendly enviornment
  • Do not let a heated customer force you to lose your cool

Attentiveness

  • The ability to really listen to customers is vital
  • Dealing wuth customers daily can get tiring on your end, but for them it is a new experience each time
  • Pay attention to individual customer interactions
  • Take note of their verbal and body language that they use to describe their problems
  • Be respectful of their feedback

Knowledge

  • The best representatives will have a deep knowledge of their company, product, enviornment
  • Without knowing the product they are having trouble with, you won't know how to help problem solve their problems.

Positive Attitude

  • The tone of your voice carries over the phone
  • Helps build your professional reputation

Troubleshooting Basics

  1. Make sure your information is safe
    • safe = your data is still available to transfer if something happens to your current computer
  2. Review what you know
    • Write down notes on what occurred
  3. Establish a theory of probable cause
    • Ask more detailed questions that lead you to a guess as to what happened
    • Research resources
  4. Test the theory to determine cause, retest as necessary
    • Test, re-test, test again
  5. Document findings, actions, and outcomes.
    • Recording the experience may be able to help solve a similar problem in the future

Clauses

A clause:
a group of words with both a subject and a verb

A subject:
The noun that is doing or being in a sentence. In other words, who/what the sentence is about

A noun:
a person, place, thing, or idea

A verb:
an action, a word that indicates a state of being


Independent Clauses

  • Independent clauses are a final thought and are able to stand on their own. Independent clauses are also known as main clauses. An independent clause contains all the information necessary to be a complete sentence. They each have a subject that tells you who or what the sentence is about and a verb that tells you what the subject is doing.

Dependent Clauses

  • A dependent clause, also known as a subordinate clause, is a clause that cannot stand on its own. It is not a complete thought because it does not contain all the information necessary to be a full sentence. Sometimes, dependent clauses start with words such as after, because, although, before, in order to, since, and though.

Visual Literacy

A few guiding questions:

  • What do you see? This is where you state the obvious!
  • What is the placement of any objects? How are they in relation to other parts of the picture?
  • This deals with the composition. What do you see in the background? In the foreground? Where are the objects placed like that instead of somewhere else?
  • What is the tone of the picture? Why does it feel like that to you?
  • Describe colors you see. What emotions are the evoking?
  • Is there any special texture? or lack of texture?
  • What do you think is the meaning of the image? What is its message? What should the viewer do as a result of looking at the image?
