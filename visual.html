Visual Literacy

What is visual literacy and why is it so important? Well, we define visual literacy as being able to analyze and interpret images. Visual literacy is a crucial skill because it is part of the foundation necessary for advanced reading and writing skills. Being able to make sense of what you see allows your critical thinking skills to expand.

The Basic Steps

So how do you practice visual literacy? Well, the good thing is, images are everywhere! Advertisements, posters, paintings, book covers- any still or even moving image is a perfect opportunity to practice visual literacy. Answer these sample guiding questions to extract and interpret as much information as you can. You can answer these questions by yourself or with other people- the more ideas the better you can understand different points of view.

What do you see? This is where you state the obvious!

What is the placement of any objects? How are they in relation to other parts of the picture? This deals with the composition. What do you see in the background? In the foreground? Where are the objects placed like that instead of somewhere else?

What is the tone of the picture? Why does it feel like that to you?

Describe colors you see. What emotions are the evoking?

Is there any special texture? or lack of texture?

What do you think is the meaning of the image? What is its message? What should the viewer do as a result of looking at the image?

Of course, there are hundreds more questions you can think of answering while interpreting an image, but use the above as a warm up exercise in visual literacy.
